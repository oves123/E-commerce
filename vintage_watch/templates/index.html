Vintage Watch History

Peter Henlein Locksmithing and clockmaker

Peter Henlein (also spelled Henle or Hele)[1] (1485 - August 1542), a locksmith, clockmaker, and watchmaker of Nuremberg, Germany. Due to the Fire-gilded pomander-shaped watch from 1505, he is often considered the inventor of the pocket watch.[2][3] He was one of the first craftsmen to make small ornamental portable clocks which were often worn as pendants or attached to clothing,[4] and which are regarded as the first watches. Many sources also erroneously credit him as the inventor of the mainspring.
